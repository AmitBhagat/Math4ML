🎯 Probability · Bayes' Theorem & MLE

Bayes' Theorem and Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE) for ML Engineers

In the realm of Machine Learning, understanding how we update our beliefs with data and how we find the "best" parameters for our models is crucial. Bayes' Theorem and MLE provide the mathematical foundation for these tasks.

1. Conditional Probability: The Foundation

Conditional Probability is the likelihood of an event occurring, given that another event has already occurred.

The Formula

$$P(A|B) = \frac{P(A \cap B)}{P(B)}$$

Intuition

Imagine you are looking at a dataset of emails. \(P(\text{Spam})\) is the probability any email is spam. However, if you know the email contains the word "Winner," you are no longer looking at the entire pool of emails; you have restricted your "universe" to only those containing that word. \(P(\text{Spam}|\text{Winner})\) is the proportion of that new, smaller universe that is actually spam.

2. Bayes' Theorem

Bayes' Theorem allows us to reverse conditional probabilities. It is the engine behind Bayesian Inference, used to update the probability of a hypothesis as more evidence becomes available.

The Formula

$$P(H|D) = \frac{P(D|H) \cdot P(H)}{P(D)}$$

Key Components

  • Posterior \(P(H|D)\): What we want to know (e.g., Probability the model parameters are correct given the data).
  • Likelihood \(P(D|H)\): How well the parameters explain the observed data.
  • Prior \(P(H)\): Our belief before seeing the data.
  • Evidence \(P(D)\): The total probability of the data under all possible hypotheses.

3. Likelihood vs. Probability

While the terms are often used interchangeably in casual conversation, they have distinct mathematical meanings in statistics.

FeatureProbabilityLikelihood
FocusPredicting outcomes.Estimating parameters.
Fixed ElementParameters \((\theta)\) are fixed.Observed Data \((x)\) is fixed.
Variable ElementData \((x)\) is the variable.Parameters \((\theta)\) are the variable.
GoalGiven the model, how likely is this data?Given this data, how likely is this model?
💡
Example: If you flip a coin 10 times and get 7 heads, the Probability describes the chance of getting that result if the coin is fair. The Likelihood function helps us determine if the coin is "fair" or "biased" based on those 7 heads.

4. Maximum Likelihood Estimation (MLE)

MLE is a method used to estimate the parameters of a probability distribution by maximizing a likelihood function.

How it works

1
Define the Likelihood Function: For a set of i.i.d. data points \(X = \{x_1, x_2, ..., x_n\}\), the likelihood is the product of individual probabilities: \[L(\theta) = \prod_{i=1}^{n} P(x_i | \theta)\]
2
Log-Likelihood: Because products are hard to differentiate, we take the natural log \((\ln)\). This turns the product into a sum, which is mathematically easier and doesn't change the location of the maximum: \[\ell(\theta) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \ln P(x_i | \theta)\]
3
Maximize: We find the derivative with respect to \(\theta\), set it to zero, and solve: \[\frac{d}{d\theta} \ell(\theta) = 0\]

ML Application

🔗
Key Insight: In Linear Regression, when we assume the residuals (errors) follow a Normal Distribution, minimizing the Mean Squared Error (MSE) is mathematically equivalent to finding the MLE for the model weights.
